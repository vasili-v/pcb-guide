# Detour: Validating the Unknowns

Taking a detour to simulate a circuit block and test it on a bench is an effective way to eliminate design uncertainties before committing to a full layout. This validation process bridges the gap between merely an idea of a circuit behavior and detailed understanding. A MOSFET-based reverse-polarity protection circuit serves as an ideal example for this exercise, offering a straightforward yet critical sub-circuit to demonstrate the complete workflow.
